This project proposes an online detection method based on SE (2) symmetry residual to address the stability degradation that may occur in two-dimensional robotic arms controlled by neural networks under conditions such as collision, jamming, actuator degradation, and sensor drift. The method takes a desktop two-dimensional robotic arm as the object, places its pose and motion trajectory within the framework of Lie group SE (2), and designs the controller as a neural network with geometric equivariance. The detector jointly utilizes three types of indicators: symmetry residual, invariant drift, and control response inconsistency to monitor the system's operating status online; When the abnormal indicators continue to exceed the threshold, the system triggers an alarm and switches to safety control mode. The core idea of this project is that normal mechanical motion should maintain structural consistency under group action, while accidents usually manifest as the consistency of this structure being disrupted. This framework takes into account the geometric structure of mechanical systems, the representation capability of neural network control, and the engineering requirements for safety monitoring during operation. The research on symmetry reduction of mechanical systems, equivariant learning of robots, and runtime monitoring provides a theoretical and methodological foundation for this article. The current simulation adopts simplified dynamics and MLP controller, mainly used to verify the feasibility of detection logic; The ideal SE (2) equivariant network controller in the paper will be updated in subsequent hardware experiments or the next version.
